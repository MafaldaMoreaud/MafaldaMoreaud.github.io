The Eye and the Mind—Perceptions
Dimensions A6 / Poster 90 x 1500mm / 2 gifs
Printed on a plotter
Toulouse—December 2017
This project is an experiment on the theme of perceptions and vision. How can vision translate what we perceive into action?
What are the limits between the visible, the "possibilities" of the vision, the perceptible and the perceived? To what extent
will the interpretation, the feeling of the person and what she knows about a place, influence the perception she has of it
and the transcription she will be able to make of it? This project deals with the relationship to places, to space, but also to the idea
of the present moment, of what marks a moment in time and space and which « defines", in a certain way, when it begins and when
it ends. This is perhaps what one might call paradoxically a kind of constant ephemeral experience … Lights are a key element of these
experiences in space. They signal it, make it special and project us into different worlds and atmospheres. The limits of translation add
to this production and onto all forms of interpretations which follow from what I am are projected my perception of this space.
No matter how hard I try to represent the perceived moment, I will never really be able to reproduce it in its entirety.
On the one hand because this moment implies a movement and a position which themselves induce a temporality. Moreover these short
moments are special precisely because they are limited in time. Consequently, my sensitivity, and all that I project in it, will unconsciously
influence the form that I give to this translation. It would be like a a total fabrication of what is real, if we can talk about
reality at all. In the end, everything is about perception and not about reality. The series deals with the the theme of interior / exterior
and explores a certain range of possibilities over terms of lights and colors. The micro animations performed give the impression
of a change in time, movement. The light seems to turn in space. It is an attempt to imagine what this moment might have been.
They have to be watched together to experience the total effect.
« What’s the point, when it’s all about
realising that our eyes are already much
more than receivers for lights, colors and lines:
computers of the world, and who have the gift
of the visible as we say that the inspired man

is earned by practise, and it is not in a few months,
nor is it in solitude that a painter comes into
possession of his vision.
That is not the question: early or late,
spontaneous or formed in the museum, his vision
