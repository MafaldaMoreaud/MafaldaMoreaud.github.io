Gérard Depeyris—Les savoir-faire
Dimensions 288 x 384—Micro edition A5
Printed on 80g textured paper
Toulouse—October 2017
Project based on the theme of know-how and its future. This edition reports a meeting with Gérard Depeyris. It aims to bring
a different perspective on the know-how and encourages us to reappropriate time. The time to meet, share, exchange, enjoy, watch,
listen, produce,experiment, change, make, undo and redo… What emerges from this meeting is the very close link between Gérard's
philosophy of life, the way he practices his profession and being a surfer himself. The three are indissociable and occupy the same place.
This consideration encourages us to treat the text, the image and the sketch with the same importance. In any case, to consider
the typographic character or the typographical gray as a visual material. Contrasts are set up thanks to the texts as well as two different
relation to these. There is Gerard's speech and then the reported speech. And so, there is some kind of a game of back and forth between
his words and mine that can intermittentlydynamize the visual. Through this information processing, a spatiality is also transcribed, linked to
the movements, but also to the displacements of the body required in the manufacturing of a board and to the rhythm of the production that
we find in gestures. The text enclosed in a column too narrow for the chosen type size, entrails an unusual and usually "forbidden" flag effect…
Through this treatment, the eye travels through the typographic blanks. The text then takes on a more poetic dimension that works with
the contemplative aspect desired for this edition. There are several levels of reading, one "functional" then another wich is purely poetic
and contemplative. I evoke a contemplative dimension because in my eyes surfing belongs to another temporality. A different temporality
to move, to look, to apprehend an environment. In this sport as in the profession of shaper, the eye, the visible, the perceived or even
the perceptible are of great importance. The eye decodes, and allows us to transcribe in gestures and forms. The pages are not connected
to each other. The reader can manipulate them together, as they were conceive, but also alone or in a very random order. In this case, what
happens? Are connections made? Is the mood still there? The photographs of the edition are not illustrations of the text. They are part
of the encounter, they tell another story. A story without words, just feelings, colors and moods. The sketches provide details mainly
of the characteristic gestures. Despite the fact that the text is treated as a material, a "sensitivity" is still brought to Gérard's speech
and phrasing. Some cuts are not insignificant. They make sense, support the meaning of certain words, the rhythm of speech, or the tone used.
